Mars

Mars is known as the Red Planet because of its reddish surface caused by iron oxide. It is a rocky planet with
mountains, valleys, and signs that water may have existed in the past.

DETAILS
Distance from the Sun: Approximately 228 million kilometers
Rotation period: About 24.6 hours
Number of moons: 2 (Phobos and Deimos)
Atmosphere: Thin atmosphere, mostly carbon dioxide

Jupiter

Jupiter is the largest planet in the solar system and is a gas giant. It is famous for its Great Red Spot, a
massive storm that has existed for hundreds of years.

DETAILS
Distance from the Sun: Approximately 778 million kilometers
Rotation period: About 9.9 hours
Number of moons: More than 90
Atmosphere: Thick atmosphere made mainly of hydrogen and helium

Saturn

Saturn is a gas giant best known for its large and beautiful ring system. It is mostly made of hydrogen and
helium and has many moons.

Details
Distance from the Sun: Approximately 1.43 billion kilometers
Rotation period: About 10.7 hours
Number of moons: More than 80
Atmosphere: Thick atmosphere, mainly hydrogen and helium
